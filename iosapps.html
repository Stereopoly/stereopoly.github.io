Make School

In the summer of 8th grade, I went to an 8 week long camp called Make School. It was a summer long class where we would first learn Swift, then ship an app to Apple's App Store. The camp was very selective and I had to go through quite a long admissions process to get in. There was a very long form with all sorts of questions to be filled out, and then a interview with the founder of the camp. I got in and loved it. The camp was located at 3 different locations: San Fransisco, New York City, and Sunnyvale. I went to the Sunnyvale locaiton which was hosted at the Plug and Play Tech Center. That location was amazing because the building housed over 300 startups. The first 3 weeks of the camp I learned Swift by cloning 2 applications that were already on the app store. Then for the next 5 weeks, I worked on my own application to be submitted to the app store. The camp culminated with a Demo Day where we showed our apps to companies and investors.
